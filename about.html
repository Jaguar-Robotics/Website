Our Story.


Team 7415 Jaguar Robotics is the FIRST Robotics Competition team of deToledo High School in West Hills, California. Founded to bring competitive robotics to deToledo students, we've grown into a team that competes and earns recognition across Southern California.

Every January, we receive the game challenge at kickoff and have exactly six weeks to design, fabricate, wire, and program a fully functional competition robot from scratch — mirroring real-world engineering workflows under intense time pressure.

Our team is entirely student-led, supported by dedicated mentors from industry and academia. We don't just build robots — we build engineers, problem-solvers, and future leaders.

We are a proud member of Team Anvil, a Southern California FRC alliance promoting collaboration, knowledge-sharing, and community growth across regional teams.

The Program

About FIRST.


FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) is a non-profit organization founded by Dean Kamen to inspire young people to pursue careers in STEM.

The FIRST Robotics Competition is the flagship high school program, challenging teams to design and build robots for a new game challenge every year. With over 3,800 teams worldwide, it is one of the most prestigious high school robotics competitions on the planet.
